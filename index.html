Ray-Ban Meta Smart Glasses

The Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses (2nd generation, 2023) are a collaboration between Meta and EssilorLuxottica. They look and feel like regular Ray-Bans — available in Wayfarer, Headliner, and other classic frames — while packing a 12 MP ultrawide camera, five-mic array for spatial audio capture, and open-ear speakers. The 2024 update added a live-streaming feature to Instagram and Facebook, and Meta AI voice assistant support, letting you ask questions about what you're seeing hands-free. Battery lasts around 4 hours of active use, with the charging case extending that to roughly 32 hours total.

XREAL Air 2

The XREAL Air 2 (released October 2023) are AR glasses focused on delivering a large personal display experience. They project the equivalent of a 330-inch screen at 4 meters, making them well-suited for watching video, working with a virtual monitor, or gaming on the go. Compared to the original Air, the Air 2 improves brightness to 500 nits, reduces the electrochromic lens tint response time, and cuts weight slightly. They connect via USB-C to phones, laptops, and consoles — no battery or Wi-Fi required. The XREAL Beam accessory adds a standalone 3DoF spatial computing mode without needing a host device.

Even Realities G1

The Even Realities G1 are prescription-compatible smart glasses with a discreet Micro-LED waveguide display visible only to the wearer. Unlike AR headsets, the G1 prioritises all-day wearability — they look like ordinary glasses and weigh under 40 g. The green monochrome HUD shows notifications, navigation, translations, AI responses, and a teleprompter, projected at roughly 2 meters in front of you to reduce eye strain. The companion app (iOS & Android) handles AI queries via Even AI, and the glasses support BLE 5.2 for a stable phone connection. Battery lasts up to 1.5 days on a charge. Note: the G1 has been succeeded by the Even G2, but remains available.
